Plagiarism

Plagiarism is considered improper use of content that is protected by copyright. Any work that you refernce in your papers or other mediums of content need to attribute the author's whose work you used. If this is not done the author/ owner of the content you used can either take your work or recieve any profits you have from publishing it.

Citations

Citations are a way for you to tell anyone viewing your work that you have recieved the information from someone else's work/research. To properly cite something you also need to provide a way for the viewer to see the orginal work that gathered information from.

Copyright

Copyright protects authors' works and makes it so in order for others to use these works they need to either purchase a license from the company or owner or alternatively you can mention where you reference your source by citing in in-text and also citing it at the end of your piece of writing/ content. There are many types of things defended by copyright most of which are listed on the main body of text in this website. For those wondering how long copyright lasts, well it is 70 years after the author's death. After this duration of time the work becomes public and anyone can use it without having to cite it. There are also some different rules for different piece of work so I would suggest reading this website for more info.

What is Copyright?

Copyright © is a law that protects the intelectual property of an author. It is a way for people to claim authorship of their work which can be: literary works, dramatic works, photographs, grpahics, computer programs, architectural designs and any other medium.



What does copyright protect?

You can copyright any piece of original work that you publish. Everything that is considered original can be copyrighted under most circumstances. A short list of examples includes:


  • Musical pieces
  • Photographs
  • Illustrations
  • Research
  • Literary works
  • Computer programs
  • Architectural designs

Something that you should note is that copyright doesn't protect: processes, concepts, principles or discoveries. These can be publically used without the need for a license and noone has copyright over these subjects.



What is plagiarism?

Some of the people in this website might be students and have heard the term plagiarism quite frequently. Plagiarism refers to using other people's work and calling it your own be it for personal profit or a simple school assignment. It can be intentional or unintentional but it is wrong regardless. In order to make sure we aren't at risk at misusing other's work we need to undestand hot to cite works that we use. There are different types of citations but the genral rule is that you need to incldue a date of the paper that you are referncing, a link and of course the name of the author. There is much more that goes into citing works but these are the basics of what to include in a citation.

Other than simply citing another person's work you also need to make sure you cite where in your paper you used the information. These are called in-text citation and they are more simple to do than regular citations. However you must cite the work at the end of your paper so anyone readin gyour work has a reference of where to go to for more details on the information that you pulled form the website/book/research paper.



How can we use some else's work?

Depending on the type of work that we are using and the medium we are using it in there are different rules we must obide by. There are two main examples I'm sure everyone can easily visualize. First is a citation in your school work and second is a license to use content in a YouTube video. Citations are mostly used in research papers and written assignments and licenses are used for content like videos or music. There is also a new kind of ownership license over digital art called NFT but that isn't too important to the topic at hand. NFTs are just tokens of ownerships over digital art products.
